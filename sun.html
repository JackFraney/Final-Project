Star Profile: The Sun

The Sun

  • Type: Yellow Dwarf Star (G2V)
  • Age: 4.6 Billion Years
  • Core Temp: 27 Million °F
  • Mass: 99.86% of the Solar System

About The Sun

The Sun is a hot ball of glowing gases at the heart of our solar system. Its gravity holds the solar system together, keeping everything—from the biggest planets to the smallest particles of debris—in its orbit.

The connection and interactions between the Sun and Earth drive the seasons, ocean currents, weather, climate, radiation belts and auroras. Though it is special to us, there are billions of stars like our Sun scattered across the Milky Way galaxy.
