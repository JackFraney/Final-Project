Planet Profile: Uranus

Uranus

  • Planet Type: Ice Giant
  • Distance from Sun: 1.8 billion miles (19.2 AU)
  • Year Length: 84 Earth Years
  • Number of Moons: 28

About Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun. It was the first planet found with the aid of a telescope, discovered in 1781 by astronomer William Herschel.

Uranus is characterized by its extreme axial tilt; it practically rotates on its side. It is categorized as an "ice giant" because most of its mass is a hot, dense fluid of "icy" materials (water, methane, and ammonia) above a small rocky core. The methane gives the planet its signature blue-green color.
